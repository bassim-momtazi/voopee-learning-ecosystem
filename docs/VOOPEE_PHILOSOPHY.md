# Theoretical Foundations of the Voopee Learning Ecosystem

## Introduction

The school is a legacy of human civilization, and its immense impact on all that defines and characterizes contemporary humanity can never be ignored. Nevertheless, throughout history, no obstacle has stood so vast and pervasive against the force of human creativity and innovation. It may seem strange, but all that has manifested as the explosive growth of science and technology in the current and past centuries has been the result of escaping the inherent limitations of scholastic education, rather than the expansion of knowledge transferred by the school to the student. One need only look at the relationship between the most influential contemporary scientists, inventors, and innovators and the institution of the school to recall how they rebelled against it to create new realms and outcomes in science and technology. Reflecting on this type of relationship—this rebellion and its remarkable results—strengthens the question: **Has the time not come to redesign and reinvent a new structure for human learning?** It seems that any delay or conservatism in this process will be cast aside, and it will not be long before the institution of the school has no place or function other than as an ancient artifact in the museum of human culture and civilization.

This discourse reflects an effort to create a new learning structure. An effort that presents a new philosophy and framework for learning and, from a practical standpoint, can, with the speed and scale of a formidable whirlwind, dismantle the logic and structure of the current educational system and replace it with a structure that is deceptively simple, inclusive, and possesses an unimaginable penetration rate. To better understand this new structure, we must redefine the concept of learning. So, let us consider ourselves as newborn infants, a time when our every small movement is an attempt to understand an unknown world and to cope with its ocean of ambiguities. In the hierarchy of needs theory presented by Maslow in 1943, which continues to be a subject of attention and critique, survival-oriented needs are presented as the first human needs and the first tier of Maslow’s famous pyramid. It is strange that in this theory, a more fundamental and universal human need—**the need for learning**—is not mentioned. Without learning, would we succeed in meeting even our most basic needs for survival? Is not the prerequisite for fulfilling the needs of the very first tier of Maslow’s pyramid learning how to satisfy them? For instance, if, in early infancy, we do not succeed in identifying our mother, understanding her supportive characteristics, and learning how to communicate with her and the techniques to summon her in various situations of primary need, would we succeed in ensuring our survival? Careful consideration of this example and the effort to generalize it leads us to the understanding that it is the need for learning (in a general sense) and its continuous development that holds inherent and practical precedence over any other need at all stages of life, for learning is the process of cognitive and functional improvement. It is a process that has been continuous and uninterrupted since birth, and even before.

Despite this, our behavior (as designers and officials of educational systems) towards this self-evident and primary need has not only never been commensurate with its importance, universality, and agency, but we have, in fact, inverted reality by attempting to strip the learner of their central role and hand it over to the teacher. Thus, we have created educational systems in which the fulfillment of the real, pure, and dynamic need for learning itself is absent, replaced by restrictive and rigid educational efforts. These are efforts that suppress creativity, ignore individual differences, and store a mass of information in the students’ passive memory merely in the hope that it might be useful in the future. We have spent precious time that should have been filled with joy, pride, creativity, and self-learning on comparative, fear-inducing endeavors driven by memory-centric exams. This historical flaw and mistake stem from a wrong but established assumption: that we hold complete agency in the learning process. That it is we who must determine what the novice learns and how they learn it, because we know what is best for them better than they do themselves. Based on this short-sighted assumption, we have reduced the novice’s role in influencing the quality and quantity of their own learning to nearly zero. Consequently, the primary output of our educational factories (schools and universities) are nothing more than passive, unhappy, uncreative, disillusioned, and intensely conservative beings.

It is worth pondering the tremendous force that the need for learning generates in the human psyche and behavior in the form of passion, thirst, and effort, to believe that:

*   It is not we who teach; it is the novice who will learn, if they choose to.
*   It is not we who determine the learner’s path and guarantee its success; rather, it is the force arising from the need to learn, alongside existing possibilities and opportunities, that shapes the novice’s roadmap.

Now, let us see if, in place of the expired current educational structure, we can build a new one that:

*   Provides unlimited and free opportunities for discovery and self-learning?
*   Is experience-driven rather than knowledge-driven?
*   Offers a more efficient and fairer mechanism for quantitative and qualitative assessment of learning?
*   Is free from the anxiety of grade-based judgment and promotes the joy of experimentation and trial-and-error?
*   Lacks a fixed starting point and is not based on following a rigid, linear learning path?
*   Ensures sufficient knowledge and experience for more effective adoption of social roles without transferring a mass of pre-existing knowledge?
*   Clearly interconnects the realms of learning?
*   Significantly reduces the time required for learning programs?
*   And finally, despite providing all necessary freedoms for innovation and creativity, is completely structured, purposeful, and manageable and assessable.

In the following sections, I will first present a summary of the learning philosophy that addresses the aforementioned changes, display the paradigm shifts of the new learning structure in a table, and then proceed to elaborate on each of them. Finally, I will introduce the executive aspect of my learning philosophy in the form of a learning ecosystem, which is my proposed replacement for the concept of an educational system.

## Knowledge

In Voopee’s philosophy, knowledge consists of the governing relationships between phenomena, once they are known. These relationships may exist between primary phenomena of existence, such as weather, celestial bodies, living beings, motion, heat, and so on, or between secondary phenomena created by humankind, such as language and script, tools, theories, laws, works of art, and more.

Relationships are the invisible and sometimes abstract threads that hold phenomena within an observable and identifiable system. Relationships can even be created between an abstract or hypothetical phenomenon or concept and other phenomena. For example, a “point” is an abstract and non-real concept that has become the foundation for creating and explaining a series of relationships known as the science of geometry.

When a relationship is discovered, assumed, or temporarily accepted, a light turns on in the mind, and an opportunity for learning is provided. It is here that a significant difference between the philosophies governing school-based educational systems and Voopee’s learning philosophy becomes clearly apparent. The current prevailing philosophies, at least at the practical level, consider the learning process to be almost complete at the stage of discovering, assuming, or accepting a relationship, and they deem its development beyond their defined responsibility. However, in Voopee’s philosophy, the stage of discovering, assuming, or accepting a relationship merely means turning on a light that should illuminate the learning arena more brightly and mark the beginning of an endless and ever-expanding process of learning. This process begins with acquiring knowledge and continues through the crystallization of insight in the mind, and skill and capability in performance.

## Learning

In Voopee’s philosophy, learning is something beyond the discovery, assumption, or acceptance of a relationship. In this philosophy, learning is a process that begins with understanding, assuming, or accepting the correctness or effectiveness of a relationship and develops into stages and degrees such as insight, skill, and capability. Therefore, what happens in current schools only overlaps with a short path of the learning process in Voopee’s philosophy. This partial overlap is also only valid at a theoretical level and is not discernible at the practical level and the reality prevailing in common schools.

As explained earlier, in the learning process, the stage of discovering, assuming, and accepting a relationship only means turning on a light that should illuminate the main learning arena as well as related peripheral areas. Immediately after this initial illumination in the dark space of the unknown, the probability of other lights turning on, which are thematically located around the first light, increases. If a collection of those other lights gradually turns on, the novice will enter a new stage of the learning process called **insight**. This additional illumination is not based on special efforts but on spark-like understandings and assumptions that arise from the learner’s pre-existing general ability to generalize and hypothesize. Now, the novice is in a much brighter area where they can venture into taking risks and practical trial-and-error for more illumination, which, if successful, signifies their entry into the **skill** stage. From this point on, they will have the courage to move along the length and breadth, and especially on the edges of light and darkness, to reach for other light switches or to benefit from the effects of the light in the realm of work and life. This is where the novice guesses the possible locations for light switches with a higher probability of correctness and acts upon it. This is where the novice experiences the acquired knowledge and becomes skilled in its relative development, and this is where, without entering it, the initial acquisition of knowledge holds little value for accepting social roles.

However, even this valuable stage of skill is not the final stage of the learning process, and to have ever more effective individuals, we need them to enter the **capability** stage. In the capability stage, the novice or “grower,” while expanding their experiences, develops the ability to theorize and personally becomes a light for illumination. They will be able to expand the learning path by their own judgment, establish links between learning domains, engage in invention and innovation, and proceed with greater independence into the unknown, excelling in creating impact and results. Capability is a stage that most of us imagine is likely to emerge only at high levels of university education. This is a perception stemming from our flawed beliefs about the concept of learning, for in every subject-specific learning process, the possibility of entering the capability stage exists. It is more dependent on the inter-subject synergies of knowledge and experience than it is directly related to factors such as age and the amount of knowledge.

## The Origin of Learning

In Voopee’s philosophy, learning is recognized as the most fundamental and universal human need. In the hierarchy of life, as we move from inanimate objects towards humans, the number of fixed and pre-programmed behavioral patterns (instinctive or reflexive behavior) decreases, while the need to create novel patterns appropriate to the capabilities of each class increases. Humans are learning from the moment of their existence. When we first receive and process a series of data from the surrounding world, and our mental organization system categorizes and develops that data, our learning process begins. For instance, the sense of touch transmits the warmth of a mother’s embrace as an electrical data stream to the brain; simultaneously, the auditory nerves send electrical data that will be interpreted as the mother’s calming voice, and vision registers an electrical interpretation of her eager and kind face in the brain. The simultaneity of these three data sets, their repetition in multiple situations, and the elimination of irrelevant additional data gradually contribute to the formation of a single template, which is later labeled “mother.” In the near future, as more data is organized and fits into this template—for example, the feeling of peace and satisfaction resulting from feeding at the mother’s breast—it will ultimately lead to the development and crystallization of the “mother” mental template within the realm of concepts such as supporter, companion, safe haven, generator of good feelings, and so on.

On the other hand, and simultaneous with receiving the initial data, our system of primitive and reflexive reactions, such as expelling air through the larynx to produce unintelligible sounds, is also activated. In a short time, the mind creates and registers a behavioral pattern from the exchange of these actions and reactions, leading to this learning:

> Are you hungry? Are you upset? Do you miss her? Make a sound and call for your mother.

**A Summary of a Learning Process:**
Data Input -> Creating a Mental Template -> Discovering the Relationship between the Mental Template and Behavioral Actions/Reactions -> Using the Discovered Relationship to Create New Behavior (Patterning) -> Further Application, Development, and Refinement of the Pattern through Trial and Error throughout Life.

In this process, there are at least four determining factors:
1.  **Intensity of Need**
2.  **Relevance of Data to the Need**
3.  **Quality of Organization**
4.  **The Catalyst (Teacher, Encourager, Guide, Companion, etc.)**

Among these four factors, the first one, the **intensity of need**, acts as the driving force, the fuel, and the ultimate cause of the learning process. This is why it has garnered the main focus in Voopee’s learning philosophy, to the extent that it leads to the creation of a new vision and strategy in learning policies. Understanding the importance of this factor has led to an unparalleled focus on creating a need for the novice, and to considering game-like “making” as the best environment for learning, instead of using static starting points and linear, inflexible educational programs. This is because it is in the process of motivated and free creation that the novice confronts their need for more knowledge and skill, and sufficient fuel for their learning journey is provided.

The second factor, the **relevance of data**, is crucial in the initial design of learning opportunities, the richness of learning starting points, and enabling network-like movement across different learning domains. In fact, the learning structure must be designed to be compatible with the diversity of novices’ needs. This will be accomplished by expert, holistic designers with the help of artificial intelligence.

The third factor, the **quality of data organization**, reflects the mental logic of the template, potential disorders, and the existence of unknown models in the novice’s data processing (such as the different logic and models of processing and patterning among individuals with autism). This is where creating forms tailored to the special conditions of novices becomes important. The mastery of learning process designers over these special individual or group conditions—which in current educational systems are referred to as learning disabilities—will add to the richness of learning opportunities.

And finally, the fourth factor, the **catalyst**, is a real or virtual person who is no longer a mere transmitter of knowledge like teachers of the old system. They are a catalyst for the transfer and application of knowledge. They inspire the novice and motivate them to discover relationships and apply knowledge in real experiences. They assess the novice’s motivation and needs and suggest suitable starting points. They accompany the novice on a networked and dynamic learning path and, in doing so, likely learn a great deal themselves. The most important thing they reveal to the novice during this companionship is how to perform realistic self-assessment. Because it is the novice who must learn alone in their future life and in uncharted territories, and to be as successful as possible, they must have the best assessments of their own learning. The catalyst understands well the relationship between knowledge domains, learning content, and social roles (and will learn even more while accompanying the novice) and can thus have a richer and more constructive companionship with the novice.

## The Goal of Learning

In Voopee’s learning philosophy, the primary goal of learning is to ensure **mental well-being**. An individual who fails to develop and deepen their understanding of existence and its functions, in proportion to their innate or acquired facilities and abilities, loses their mental health. Such a situation is analogous to that of a prisoner in solitary confinement who, due to severely limited contact with the outside world, loses the opportunity to update and expand their understanding and insight, and gradually the destructive effects of this situation appear on their psyche.

The next goal in Voopee’s learning philosophy is the increasingly **effective fulfillment of social roles**. Every social role requires a certain level of knowledge, insight, skill, and capability commensurate with that role and even related roles. Individuals must possess a degree of initial readiness to accept and perform that role better, and they must develop and improve it in the course of fulfilling the role. The Voopee learning structure, with the help of artificial intelligence, is designed to give the novice the opportunity to figuratively take on social roles, so they can practically feel themselves in the position of fulfilling their chosen and accepted social role and face the upcoming issues and challenges in a simulated manner. In fact, just as a pilot uses a simulator to acquire the necessary skills for their responsibilities, in the Voopee learning structure, a specific simulation is created for each social role, including carpenter, painter, sailor, consultant, factory manager, nurse, and so on. These can, of course, range from simple interactive audio-textual displays to different and varied real-world or digital field situations. Furthermore, since in the path of social development we constantly face the rise and fall in the value and influence of existing social roles and the necessity of creating new ones, learning how to create a new social role will be one of the secondary objectives of the Voopee learning structure.

## Detailed Explanations Regarding the Paradigm Shifts

### Education vs. Learning
The prevailing paradigm clearly has an unavoidable focus on the word “education” (or “teaching”). The dominant use of this term signifies the view that the direction and agency in the process leading to learning flow from the source—the teacher and educational content—towards the student. It is as if no purposeful learning would occur without the existence of a teacher and standardized educational content. At the same time, what is encompassed by the concept of “education” in this paradigm is often the knowledge itself, not the experience of applying it. Furthermore, “knowledge” is that which has already been known, not what should or might be known. In this paradigm, the value of knowledge lies in its being previously known, not in the quality of its discovery, understanding, or the experience of its application.

In the Voopee paradigm, the word “education” has no application. In this paradigm, the word **“learning”** is highlighted, which signifies the direction of a process that flows from the source of the novice’s passion and need towards their cognitive and skill development. In this paradigm, knowledge is the discovery/understanding/acceptance of relationships between phenomena, and its value does not lie in its volume or its preservation in memory, but rather depends on the ability to apply it.

### Teacher vs. Motivator
In the prevailing paradigm, the role of the teacher is to transfer knowledge from the source of standardized educational content to the destination of the student’s memory, and then to ensure that the intended knowledge has been firmly established in the student’s mind and memory in the exact form and manner presented in the content. Given the educational structure, the teacher has very little room to maneuver to create enthusiasm and motivation, and especially to reveal the student’s need to acquire the knowledge in question, because the educational content is so vast that it leaves no time for such initiatives.

In the Voopee paradigm, we speak of a **“Motivator.”** The Motivator is a real person or a virtual one derived from artificial intelligence who, in an initial assessment, examines the novice’s ability and position in the realm of knowledge and experience. They encourage the novice to accept one of the social roles suggested to them, create opportunities for them to build or solve problems, and by doing so, prepare them to understand their need for the necessary knowledge and experience in the process of making/problem-solving. The Motivator then guides their learning process through various domains until they succeed in discovering and understanding a relationship, and then use it to build or solve the problem they faced in their social role.

### Student vs. Grower
In the prevailing paradigm, the “student” is merely a recipient of knowledge that has been formulated and standardized for potential use in their future. In this paradigm, firstly, any deepening and development of the accepted knowledge is beyond the responsibility of the teacher and the educational system. Secondly, the student has little opportunity to apply the knowledge they have received. Thirdly, they are deprived of the excitement of discovery because they must accept and, at best, understand what has been formulated for them. In this paradigm, the educational content is not designed to guide the student towards discovery/understanding, for instance, through a Socratic method; rather, the direct and rapid transfer of knowledge is what receives attention.

In the Voopee learning paradigm, the concept of a “student” has been changed to a **“Grower.”** A Grower is someone who chooses their own path of growth, encounters dynamic problems or challenges on that path, and proceeds to learn upon realizing their own need. In this endeavor, they discover a relationship or relationships, return to solve the previous problem or challenge, and experience the application of that knowledge. They do not learn something merely in the hope that it might be useful in an unknown future, yet at the same time, they have the opportunity and ability to expand their knowledge and experience in their desired domains to the limit of their momentary capacity.

### School vs. Learning Park
In the prevailing paradigm, a “school” is an environment for bringing teachers and students together so that the teacher has an opportunity and a setting to transfer knowledge to the student. The concept of a school has not undergone significant evolution throughout history and has not gone beyond physical environments with fixed forms.

In the Voopee learning paradigm, the **“Learning Park”** replaces the concept of a school. A Learning Park includes a base as a real symbol for learning efforts and administrative affairs, where a portion of the learning programs that have a collective aspect also takes place. However, the major part of the learning activities and programs varies across diverse environments, from virtual simulators of social roles (usable at home or in neighborhood centers) to real environments such as factories, laboratories, parks, stadiums, farms, and so on.

### Educational Content vs. Learning Domains
In the prevailing paradigm, “educational content” consists of a standardized volume of knowledge around a general subject like mathematics, various sciences, and skills like reading and writing, which, at the discretion of content designers in educational systems, is typically compiled and classified in a book. The most prominent feature of the selection and compilation of such content is that it cannot be mindful of individual differences; rather, it is concerned with the general range and amount of knowledge a student should have accumulated for their future academic, professional, and personal life. Another characteristic of this standardized content is its high volume, which practically leaves no opportunity for experimentation and practical use of the learned knowledge, even if the teacher and student are willing. A further feature of such content is its complete dependence on being taught by a teacher, as its format is often not based on self-learning and personal progress by the student, and very few students would be able, for instance, to go through the stages of discovery and learning on their own by studying a textbook.

In the Voopee learning paradigm, the term **“Learning Domain”** is used for different learning subjects. Learning Domains are open, expandable, interconnected, and synergistic fields that the Grower enters by their own choice and the guidance of the Motivator, and they will fully understand the connections between them. The domains are completely expandable, and the Grower can expand them both in depth and breadth. At the same time, the domains have a networked and synergistic relationship with each other, such that a Grower will understand how acquiring skill and knowledge in, for example, the mathematics domain helps their skill in sports, music, or better performing their social role as a locomotive driver.

The outstanding element of such a design is establishing a connection between the domains and social roles. Here, progressing in the depth of a part of a domain is, in fact, the application of discovered/accepted knowledge at other levels of practical experience, which are expressed with titles such as insight, skill, and capability. The domains are designed in such a way that the Grower feels that the value of expanding a domain’s depth (application of knowledge) is far greater than expanding its breadth (increase of knowledge).

With the help of artificial intelligence, all details of the Grower’s presence in the domains, the quality and quantity of their expansion, and their interconnections are recorded. This provides comprehensive and unimaginable analyses of the Grower’s potential capacities and abilities and presents a far more meaningful report card on how they have navigated their learning process.

### Assessment vs. Challenge
In the prevailing paradigm, it is necessary to conduct periodic assessments to ascertain the amount of knowledge accumulated by the student. This serves both as a motivational factor for the student to consolidate their knowledge and as a record of the amount of knowledge acquired. The structure of this type of assessment is based on reducing errors, not on increasing capabilities. Answers are categorized as right/wrong, and the goal is to reduce the number of wrong answers. These tests, alongside the prevailing competitive culture, create a basis for comparison among students, shifting their focus from their personal learning path to comparing themselves with others. Due to their periodic nature, such tests cannot reinforce the student’s learning at the moment of learning; in practice, the student reviews what they have learned long after, on the night before an exam, essentially trying to recall what has faded from memory. Such tests fundamentally strip away the possibility of student participation and collaboration, except for cheating and copying from each other’s answers. Although this is legally prohibited, it is often used extensively as the only and most superficial method of participation and cooperation. Furthermore, these tests are entirely based on the judgment of an external agent, the teacher. No effective and reliable method for self-assessment is taught to the student.

In the Voopee learning paradigm, the concept of a test is changed to the concept of a **“Challenge.”** These challenges are experience-based, not knowledge-based. In this way, in the learning domains, every successful move in depth means overcoming a challenge that signifies a level of application of knowledge and insight in the context of experience. This level of knowledge application can be one of the levels of skill and capability, each identified by a specific color. Therefore, at any moment, a single glance at the landscape of the Grower’s progress in the domains can reveal how deep they have advanced in each area of a domain and at what degree of knowledge application they stand. These challenges are proposed to the Grower at the very moment of learning, and thus, participating in them can instantly extend the discovered/accepted knowledge to deeper layers. Many challenges must be open to collaboration, so that the value of cooperation in overcoming challenges becomes increasingly apparent to the Grower. These dynamic challenges are designed and proposed by artificial intelligence, which encourages the Grower to undertake them. The choice is theirs as to what landscape of progress they wish to record—a landscape showing their progress in the deeper layers of learning or one indicating a superficial movement across the domains. These are personal visuals of the quality and quantity of knowledge and skill acquisition, each with its own beauty and story. Although they can be explained, they are so diverse and personal that they are no longer comparable to each other like report cards based on grades. This way, the thirst for comparison with others is reduced, and the possibility for self-assessment is provided. Self-assessment is a valuable skill that the Grower gradually acquires in the course of overcoming challenges, as the challenges will be designed to encourage the Grower towards ever-better performance and to always be able to compare their new performance only with their own previous performances and articulate it.

### Educational System vs. Learning Ecosystem
An educational system is a hierarchical structure where the power to implement educational policies diminishes from the apex of the pyramid towards its base. Although opportunities for minor initiatives always exist for the lower ranks and the base of the pyramid, the more controlled an educational system is, the less possibility there is for the formation of large-scale, effective models resulting from minor initiatives at the bottom. Since control is the foundation of a hierarchical system, the issue of significant delays in evaluation, model creation, and the flourishing of minor initiatives has always plagued the world’s educational systems. At the same time, since the apex of the pyramid is always either within governments or at a point controlled by the government, most educational policies cannot resist strains of political governance. Even if in the most liberal governments, controlling education through policy seems unnecessary, the very model of centralized, hierarchical control will ultimately lead to the prohibition of macro-innovations, the rewarding of more compliant elements, the marginalization of different ideas, and the orientation of knowledge towards areas of government interest.

In the Voopee learning paradigm, the educational system is a self-organizing **learning ecosystem** that, firstly, lacks the centralized management of traditional educational systems, both in the development of knowledge and in executive management. This ecosystem, through the agency of a dedicated artificial intelligence system, is always and instantly influenced by the daily development of human knowledge, has no interest in being directional (unless it is manipulated), strongly supports innovation, and records and analyzes the data generated in the learning processes with unrivaled speed and accuracy, using it to create macro-models. This is a process that might take years in the most agile educational systems with centralized management.

The creator of this learning ecosystem is an autonomous international foundation, currently being established, which is committed to reducing its managerial role after the initial launch in such a way as to guarantee its maximum autonomy within a defined timeframe, for instance, by the time the ecosystem’s artificial intelligence reaches an acceptable stage of flourishing.

In this ecosystem, real and virtual learning parks will be the visible aspect of its operation. This ecosystem is formed by the interplay of four general and main factors as follows:
1.  **The Voopee Autonomous Learning Foundation:** Its founder is the theorist and creator of this ecosystem, and the foundation itself has played and will continue to play the role of strengthening its efficiency during the incubation period and protecting its independence until the flourishing period. This foundation will also be the developer of the most key player in this ecosystem, the artificial intelligence system. After this dedicated AI achieves sufficient maturity, the foundation’s managerial role will be reduced to a minimum or redirected to other areas.
2.  **The Repository of Human Knowledge:** This is constantly developing and is accessible to the ecosystem through Voopee’s dedicated artificial intelligence system. In turn, it is fed and enriched by the data from the same system.
3.  **The Growers:** These are the individuals in whom the ecosystem’s capability for effectiveness becomes apparent and is enhanced by them. They have experienced and practiced social roles during their learning period in the ecosystem and will be the acceptors of those roles to meet social needs and enrich society. By producing knowledge and experience, they will add to the richness of human knowledge and, ultimately, to the strength and effectiveness of the ecosystem.
4.  **Society:** With all its actors, it acts as the reservoir, reference, and generator of needs and their corresponding social roles, as well as the arena for the impact and role-playing of the ecosystem’s growers. It also adds to the richness of the ecosystem through the network relationships among its own agents.

## Voopee’s Dedicated Artificial Intelligence Learning System
**(The Most Key Element of the Ecosystem)**

In its initial growth stage, this system learns to classify the repository of human knowledge and experience efficiently and in correspondence with social roles. This classification will be in a way that allows for maximum summarization for visual representation in a chart understandable to novices. The breadth and length of this chart will represent access to knowledge and insight, while its depth or height will represent access to skills and capabilities. This chart also uses a model for displaying thematic learning domains that, while showing boundaries for each domain, also has the ability to display the dynamic connections and dependencies of the domains (perhaps something similar to the neural network of the brain).

After this stage, the system learns to design and offer simple to advanced simulators for each social role, so that growers can use them to gain practical experience and face the challenges of that role.

Also, in its best state, this system produces characters in the role of a “Motivator” who communicate with the grower interactively and in real-time, accompanying them on their learning path.

This system also supports the efficiency of virtual and real learning parks, establishes coordination between them and other spaces for experience and learning such as factories, internship centers, real stadiums, or virtual applications separate from the system, and records, reports, and interprets the manner of presence and activity in them.

What this system reports as a summary of the growers’ learning processes will be a rich and valid basis for their employment in social roles. The human resources units of institutions will be able to recruit and select a workforce tailored to their needs with a confidence factor beyond current imagination. For example, by observing individuals’ learning report charts, they will find out to what extent their capabilities match the institution’s needs and, if necessary during their service, in exactly which domains and to what extent they should learn and gain experience again.

It should be noted that this system is the most key element for achieving the ultimate efficiency of the ecosystem. However, until it is established as a successful and anticipated system, it will be possible to start the life of the learning ecosystem in a real (non-virtual) form, in a representative capacity, by employing a team of skilled and creative individuals who have a good understanding of the ecosystem’s principles.
